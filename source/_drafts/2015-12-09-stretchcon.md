### Questions and notes

* May **1-percentage improvements** accumulate in a significant way? (process improvements in particular)
  * Count `1.01^365` vs `0.99^365`
  * What about working days vs full year
  * Recall the cyclists team, winning Tour de France 3 times in 4 yours.
* What does it take to introduce them?
  * Actions vs identity change
* Define the relation of Results/Actions/Identity in changing habits (based on the presentation)
  * Results > Actions > Identity
  * We're focusing on actions, rarely on the identity change
  * example with loosing weight (I want to loose weight -> take a diet -> be a person, that is working out)
* How prioritize between good and great ideas?
  * urgent vs important
  * throw away good roses to make the space for great ones
* Elaborate on the 25-5 Rule
  * list 25 top goals
  * mark the top 5
  * do not work on the rest of them at all
  * important goals are enemies of most important goals
  * Warren Buffet
* What was the short history of priority word? What does mean to you?
  * Singular for > 500 years
  * plural in XX century
* Elaborate on the 3 R's of habit formation (Reminder -> Routine -> Reward)
* Elaborate on hot triggers vs cold triggers (do smth later)
  * hot - immediate, like a link in a message
  * cold - followed up later, not immediately
* How the 2-minute Rule by David Allen may work for establishing new habits?
* How to anchor new habits in existing ones
* How to improve effect by pre-committment?
  * Call for taking flu shot vs appointment
* What is the Keystone habit? How may it help you?
  * for James it's working out
* Why should you Design for Laziness?
  * BJ Fogg - Popcorn in garage instead of the kitchen
  * Water vs soda in a cafeteria (researcherzy zmienili, 12% w dół, 25% w górę)
* What's a paper clip strategy
  * Sales calls through the day vs analysis/learning/...
  * does a Kanban board match this idea?
* How long does it take to build habit?
  * Avarage - 66 days streak, but different habits establish in different time
* What's Seinfeld Strategy?
  * Don't break the chain
